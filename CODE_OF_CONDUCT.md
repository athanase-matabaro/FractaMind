# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and harassment-free experience for everyone who contributes to or uses FractaMind, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

---

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior

We encourage:
- **Respectful communication** — Be kind, considerate, and constructive in discussions
- **Collaborative problem-solving** — Focus on solutions, not blame
- **Welcoming newcomers** — Help onboard new contributors with patience
- **Constructive feedback** — Provide specific, actionable suggestions
- **Acknowledging contributions** — Recognize the work of others

### Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not tolerated:
- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind
- Offensive comments related to personal characteristics
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments
- Publishing others' private information without permission
- Any conduct that creates an unsafe or unwelcoming environment

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all FractaMind project spaces, including:
- GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, discussions)
- Code comments and documentation
- Community events and meetups (virtual or in-person)
- Social media interactions related to the project

---

## Enforcement

### Reporting Issues

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it by:
1. **Email**: [maintainer email — to be added]
2. **GitHub**: Open a confidential issue or contact maintainers directly

All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. We are committed to maintaining confidentiality for reporters.

### Consequences

Violations may result in:
1. **Warning**: A private written warning with clarification of the violation
2. **Temporary Ban**: Temporary suspension from project spaces
3. **Permanent Ban**: Permanent removal from all project interactions

The project maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct.
